Ethnic Identity
Racial and ethnic composition of study abroad participants over time. Understanding diversity in participation is key for equity-focused program development.
Ethnic Identity by Year
Stacked area — all recorded categories
Overall Composition
Total across all 9 years
🌍
White students represent an average of ~78% of participants — a significant over-representation relative to most campus demographics.
📈
Black student participation reached 51 students in 2024–25 — the highest post-COVID figure, driven in large part by the Ghana Winter program which drew 14 Black women in a single cohort.
🌍
The Ghana Winter Experiential Learning program is the single most significant equity development in the dataset — nearly all participants were Black women, demonstrating what targeted programming can achieve.
Data Note: "Unknown" category appears in some years (up to 34 students in 2014–15) and was absent in others, suggesting inconsistent collection methods across years. Multi-Racial data was not recorded in some post-COVID years.

06
Terms of Study
When are students choosing to go? This panel breaks down participation by academic term — revealing a strong preference for Summer and Spring Break programs over full-semester commitments.
Term Participation by Year
Grouped bar chart — all 9 term types
Overall Term Preference
Total across all recorded years
☀️
Summer Semester remains the most popular term, consistently accounting for 40–52% of all participation. Students prefer shorter, lower-commitment options.
🌸
Spring Break surged to 116 students in 2024–25 — the highest since 2016–17 and a dramatic reversal of its post-COVID decline. This is the year's most notable term trend.
❄️
Winter term rebounded to 29 students in 2024–25 from just 6 in 2023–24, largely driven by the Ghana Winter program. Spring Semester also held strong at 62 students.
